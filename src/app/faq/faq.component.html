Frequently Asked Questions
The Frontiers in Flight airshow starts on Sept. 8th and will be open to the public through the 9th. The gates open at 9:00AM and closes at 4:30PM.
Frontiers in Flight is an open house, and entry to the event is free. There are vendors attending that will sell food and beverages.
A growing list of performers are listed here. Unfortunately, the sequence is not solidified until the morning of the Airshow to allow for security, safety, weather, and operational concerns.
The Schedule for Saturday and Sunday is planned to be identical. When dealing with aviation, there is always a chance the schedule might change.
The majority of static displays will be open for the entire day. The aircraft participating in both static and as aerial performers will close prior to their performance, and potentially open up after the performance is over.

Permitted Items

  1. Diaper Bags
  2. Small Purses (not to exceed one cubic foot)
  3. Wheelchairs
  4. Strollers
  5. Cameras
  6. Lawn Chairs
  7. Handheld Umbrellas
  8. Sunscreen

Do not bring these items

  1. Weapons of any kind
    1. Firearms
    2. Knives
    3. Pocket Tools
    4. Scissors
    5. Lasers
    6. Self Defense Weapons (i.e. Tasers, pepper spray, etc.)
  2. Federally Illegal Drugs
  3. Clothing with profanity, nudity, racist intent or graphic violence of any kind
  4. Clothing with the perception of impersonating military or law enforcement personnel
  5. Banners or signs of any kind
  6. Toys resembling lasers or firearms
  7. RC Aircraft (i.e. drones, balloons)
  8. Flammable Items or Fireworks
  9. Pressurized Canisters (i.e. Silly String or Spray Paint)
  10. Pets (exception for certified service animals)
  11. Outside Food and Drink (refillable water bottles are permitted)
    1. Alcoholic Beverages
    2. Coolers
    3. Glass Containers
  12. Sporting Equipment/Personal Transportation Vehicles
    1. Bicycles
    2. Skateboards
    3. Scooters
    4. Roller Blades
  13. Excessively Large Items
    1. All bags must meet be at or under 12 inches by 12 inches by 12 inches
    2. Working cameras and lens are permitted if meeting the above standards
  14. Any and all items security personnel consider a safety or security risk
