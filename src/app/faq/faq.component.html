Frequently Asked Questions
The Frontiers in Flight airshow starts on Sept. 8th and will be open to the public through the 9th. The gates open at 9:00AM and closes at 4:30PM.
Frontiers in Flight is an open house, and entry to the event is free. There are vendors attending that will sell food and beverages.
A growing list of performers are listed here. Unfortunately, the sequence is not solidified until the morning of the Airshow to allow for security, safety, weather, and operational concerns.
The Schedule for Saturday and Sunday is planned to be identical. When dealing with aviation, there is always a chance the schedule might change.
The majority of static displays will be open for the entire day. The aircraft participating in both static and as aerial performers will close prior to their performance, and potentially open up after the performance is over.

Permitted Items

  1. Diaper Bags for individuals with small children or special needs and small purses not to exceed 1 cubic foot
  2. Strollers, wheelchairs and electric scooters
  3. Cameras, and/or Camcorders (without bags)
  4. Lawn chairs

McConnell Air Force Base is a federal military installation, which requires a higher level of security and stricter law enforcement than a normal public venue. All individuals, vehicles, and items are subject to search and seizure to access the installation. Please expect TSA/airport level security at all gates and access points.
